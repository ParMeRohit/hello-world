Civil Engineering

Civil engineering is a professional engineering discipline that deals with the design, construction, and maintenance of the physical and naturally built environment, including public works such as roads, bridges, canals, dams, airports, sewerage systems, pipelines, structural components of buildings, and railways.

Demand for biomedical, civil engineers continues to rise. Employment of civil engineers is projected to grow 20 percent from 2012 to 2022, according to the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics.

In summary, Civil engineering would be a good career choice. Any basic engineering field is good for your career at all times. Specifically, civil engineering also has the advantage of getting a government job in almost every sector.

Career After Civil Engineering

Building control surveyor

Building control surveyors are also known as building control officers (BCOs). They guide and give assistance while building new structures and demolitioning old ones, keeping safety in mind.Building control surveyors also check the process of construction during and after the completion to ensure that regulatory standards of safety, sustainability, accessibility and design are met. Building control surveyors are required in every small to big structures, such as extending a private bungalow or building a new shopping mall.

CAD Technician

Computer-aided design systems are used to create design plans for structures such as machines and buildings. Technician who use this technology is called CAD technician . This technology basically eliminates the age old process of drawing with pen on paper and replaces it with technical drawings using electronic machines. Though it is computer-aided designing, technicians need to use their mathematical and engineering knowledge along with skills to design buildings, machinery, products and parts.

Consulting Civil Engineer

A Consulting Civil Engineer can work as a freelancer, work for a consulting firm or even work for a construction company. Basic job of a Consulting Civil Engineer is to design, plan and manage civil construction projects, covering all categories of residential and commercial construction. The professionals need to have management skills in addition to engineering and professional trainings.

Constructing Civil Engineer

In the public and private sectors the infrastructures are conceived, designed, built, supervised, operated, constructed, and maintained by the Construction Civil Engineers. Infrastructures such as airports, bridges, roads, buildings, tunnels, dams, systems for water supply and sewage treatment. In terms of education, students concentrate on construction procedures, methods, costs, schedules and personnel management eventually to be skilled in completing a project budget and of the desired quality.

Design Engineer

A Design Engineer's job is to utilise his/her creativity and design a product to be manufactured for use. Design Engineer's study, research and develop ideas to come up with new products and systems. They also work on existing products to amplify its use and profitability. Design Engineers work on almost every consumer product imaginable for large-scale and small-scale production, from television sets and medical equipment to kitchen appliances and car engines.

Nuclear Engineer

Nuclear Engineers research and find out ways to use nuclear energy and radiation in the form of instruments and systems. To seep benefits, nuclear engineers find uses for radioactive materials in industrial and medical uses. For example, equipment used in medical diagnosis and treatment of cancer. Some also find the use of Nuclear energy to increase the military power of an Army called as nuclear weapons. Apart from designing and developing of nuclear equipment, Nuclear Engineers also direct maintenance activities of operational nuclear power plants to ensure that they meet safety standards.

Site Engineer

A Site Engineer's job is to mark out the site, make sure designs are applied correctly and liaise with main and subcontractors and the site manager. As a part of site management team, responsibilities include security, health, safety, organising and supervising materials and people. Also the responsibilities including solving technical issues, providing advice, management and preparing reports. Most times a Bachelor's degree in Civil Engineering is enough, however some big companies might require master's degree too.

Structural Engineer

To become a successful Structural Engineer, one needs to have a solid foundation in physics and mathematics such as geometry, trigonometry and calculus. A Structural Engineer's job is to design, plan and oversee the construction of new buildings, bridges, tunnels and other structures. They could also help in the alterations and extensions to existing properties. They must ensure that their designed structure must endure stresses and pressures inflicted through human use and environmental conditions. Structural Engineer are trained to operate AutoCAD too.

Water Engineer

Water engineers design, plan, supply, manage and maintain clean water and sewage water services and work on water treatment and flood defence structures such as pump systems and pipe networks. Apart from writing reports, monitoring flood levels and managing project budgets their responsibilities also include liaising with clients, contractors, government agencies, local authorities and suppliers. Water engineers can also earn a Master's Degree in Water Systems Engineering within 2years.
