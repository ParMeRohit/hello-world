M.Tech in mechanical engg

After scoring a minimum of 55% in B.tech and completing graduation, a student can pursue M.Tech in Mechanical engineering to obtain a Masters Degree. This is a 2 years program and 3 years if chosen to study part-time. The Fee for Master's in Mechanical engineering could cost anywhere between 40 Thousand to 1 lacs depending on the Institute. Students who have interest and enjoy the designing, making and installation process of machines can opt for this course. Masters of Technology in Mechanical engineering teaches one the in depth knowledge of machines using science and technology.

Piping design and engineering course

Piping Engineering is basically the designing and construction of fluids and gases through the system of pipes. It is a very crucial and daunting task to get the piping systems right and can be time consuming too. Piping design engineers are required from start to end of the job as they have to inspect the site, draw blueprints, design and manage cost for building as well and also decide on materials to be used. Common example of piping engineering is found everywhere i.e Plumbing.

Master of engineering in Tool Design

Master of engineering in Tool Design is basically the study of planning, manufacturing, execution and development of related tools and machines specially for industrial purposes to produce particular products with minimum labor, time and materials. This is a 2 year course after graduation. Though the course fee ranges between INR 2 and 5 Lacs, depending on the institute, the annual salary after the course completion and placement in a company could be anywhere between INR 4 to 15 Lacs. Job opportunities could be as Researcher, Executive Engineer, Mechanical Design Engineer and Lecturer among other job profiles.

Robotics course

Minimum 60% marks in Bachelors of Engineering or Technical is required to apply for Robotics course. Being a Mechanical engineer is an advantage in learning Robotics Course. This course basically teaches one the designing, construction and operation of Robots. Robotic Course will teach extended knowledge of mechanical engineering, kinematics and dynamics, electrical engineering, control systems and software engineering. There are two types of learning, theoretical and technical hands-on courses.

Mechatronics course

Mechatronics course is a combination of principles from electronics, mechanics, robotics, computer engineering, and automatic control, hence it is an interdisciplinary engineering field. In Mechatronics course one is taught to use the above knowledge and apply skills to make economic, reliable, and simplified industrial robots. PC hard drives, Anti-lock brakes and CD players all some of the hybrid forms of mechatronics. After completing the course, one can find jobs such as Automation engineer, Electronics design engineer and others.

Nanotechnology

"Nanotechnology is science, engineering, and technology conducted at the nanoscale, which is about 1 to 100 nanometers." This is the most simple and apt definition of Nanotechnology. Recently about 30 years ago the microscopes needed to see things at the nanoscale were invented hence Nanotechnology is the ability to see and to control individual atoms and molecules. Nanotechnology offers the potential for new and faster kinds of computers, more efficient power sources and life-saving medical treatments.

Masters in business administration

A Master of Business Administration (MBA) is by definition a general management degree designed to educate students in areas such as finances, human resources, marketing, strategy and operations management. In this Degree, a graduate is provided with theoretical and practical training to help gain a better understanding of general business management functions and human behaviour understanding to run a large or small business. This is a 2 year course. Apart from training on finance, accounting, marketing, and business law, Management training is of most importance in any MBA curriculum.
