Architect

An architect is a person who plans, designs and reviews the construction of buildings. To practice architecture means to provide services in connection with the design of buildings and the space within the site surrounding the buildings that have human occupancy or use as their principal purpose.

Foresight is a prerequisite as designing buildings is a very complex and demanding undertaking.

The architect, once hired by a client, is responsible for creating a design concept that both meets the requirements of that client and provides a facility suitable to the required use. The architect must meet with, and question, the client in order to ascertain all the requirements (and nuances) of the planned project.

Assistant Urban Designer

In simple terms, Urban Designing is city planning for citizens to coexist with nature in cities. An urban designer, designs buildings, parks, spaces and landscapes, and establish the process that make successful development possible and an Assistant Urban Designer simply assists in all the job responsibilities of an Urban Designer. The openings in these sectors are both public and private companies, and you will gain exposure to a range of responsibilities including surveying land, expanding or renovating a facility to meet current needs or even developing a transportation network.

Junior Architectural Designer

Junior Architectural Designers will be working under the Senior Architectural Designer throughout the time period gaining experience and getting better in their job. This position offers a great start for aspiring architects. Junior Architectural Designers assist in the construction of architectural projects. They get the documents required, plan designs and write building plans and assist in the presentation of materials. They need to be experts in using AutoCAD and Revit. They need to be skilled in Thinking Creatively, Drafting, Laying Out, communication, computer skills among others.

Project Architect

Project Architects manage construction projects from its 1st stage to planning to its last stage of construction. Basic responsibilities of a Project Architect is Planning, designing, drafting, communicating project matters to the concerned authorities and clients. There is a huge responsibility on them to finish the project on time with cost-effectiveness and safety guidelines while overlooking the teams work during the entire process of construction.

Residential Architect

Residential Architects plan, design overlook construction process specially only for the residential properties. They have the flexibility to work for contractors or for homeowners on individual projects as self-employed or freelancers. Unline, government or other commercial constructions, their planning is mostly depended on the client requirements, budgets and are of land. To work in International waters, one needs to pass the ARE i.e Architect Registration Examination, administered by the NCARB.

Graphic Designer

Graphic Designer uses graphic methods for communication and problem-solving projects such as use of typography, photography, motion graphics, and illustration. To present their ideas on projects, Graphic designers create visual content using computer software or by hand drawing. There are numerous job opportunities for Graphic designers as they are required to make advertisements, brochures, magazines, web designs and corporate reports. Due to the varied skills gained during graduation with a degree in architecture, they can apply their planning, problem-solving and design skills in a range of environments.
