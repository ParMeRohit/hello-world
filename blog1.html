Programming

Coder• Programmer• Codes Apps and softwares

A computer programmer's job is to code and decode the designed programs into computer languages for proper functioning of the programs and apps developed by the software designers and engineers. This job is achieved after pursuing degree with graduation in Information Technology along with doing entry level jobs for a couple of years to prove the experience. It increases the market value of a student to be certified in specific computer languages such as Java, C++, Python, PHP, etc.

Tester

Theresa Winston • Jan 18, 2019• News

As a software tester, one's job is to test computer programs or software designs for any errors or bugs with functional testing, customer scenario testing, data loading testing and many more automatic and manual test, at the programs final stages. Software tester has to make sure that the client and customers receive the functioning of the final product as intended and faultless. This technical role requires a strong understanding of SQL, a database language.

Computer Support Specialist

Theresa Winston • Jan 18, 2019• News

As a Computer Support Specialist you are required to troubleshoot and resolve multiple computer and software issues. This job could be limited to help desk or even as an employee in IT department of an organization. Further variation in this job could also have one working as a Computer Network Support Specialist troubleshooting Local Area Network (LAN's), Wide Area Networks (WAN's) and Internet systems by door-to-door service. Though same job profiles yet the Computer Support Specialists' are also known as Technical or Tech Support Specialist, Help Desk Technician, IT Specialist, Network Technician.

Harware and Network Expert

Theresa Winston • Jan 18, 2019• News

With the increased use of desktops, laptops, mobiles and Internet in private and government organizations, Computer Hardware and Networking Expert is one of the most lucrative careers in recent times. Large-scale organizations need to handle their host systems on a daily basis in the LAN and WAN networks to keep smooth exchange of data between their computers and devices, for which they are highly dependent on Hardware and Networking Expert. Traditionally a Hardware and Network professionals works on Motherboards, RAM, Modems, Routers, Firewalls and Wireless Access Points and other such devices.

Industrial Microbiologist

Theresa Winston • Jan 18, 2019• News

Industrial Microbiology or microbial biotechnology is the study of microorganisms such as bacteria, fungi, algae, protozoa and viruses or plant and animal cells with the application of scientific and engineering principles to the processing of industrial materials to create useful products or processes. For example, in the production of high-value products such as drugs, chemicals, fuels and electricity. An Industrial microbiologist may examine microbial growth found in chemical factories, oversee the microbial activities used in cheese production to ensure quality or monitor the impact industrial waste has on the local ecosystem.

Medical Microbiologist

Theresa Winston • Jan 18, 2019• News

Medical microbiologist, also known as Clinical Microbiologist diagnose, treat and prevent infections caused by microorganisms such as bacteria, fungi, viruses and parasites, through a series of analysis on samples using computer software, clinical test and range of identification methods. To see the results of their therapy, diagnosis or growth in research they are in daily contact with patients of ICU and with complex infections. This job is mostly done in medical laboratories.

Biomedical Scientist

Theresa Winston • Jan 18, 2019• News

Biomedical scientist's job is to test and analyse specimens of blood, tissues, urine and faeces to develop solutions to threatening diseases using complex and modern computer-aided testing procedures in a laboratory. A Biomedical Scientist is very important in hospital departments in treating the patients of Operation theatres, Accident and emergency (A&E) and many more to get the correct blood group and other data before treating the patient. Since this is a laboratory work, high analytical skills are required along with writing and maintaining of medical reports.

Cell Biologist

Theresa Winston • Jan 18, 2019• News

Obtaining a bachelor's degree in cellular biology is the foundation of the study of cells, including their function, structure and interaction with living organisms. Cell biologists work with high-powered electron microscopes for detailed understanding of the cells. Cell biologist also work as a microbiologist, biochemist and biophysicists. To lead or direct a research in further stages of career, a Doctorate Degree (PhD, DPhil or DSc) is important.

Bacteriologist

Theresa Winston • Jan 18, 2019• News

Bacteriology study falls under the umbrella of Microbiology. Bacteriologist specifically deals only with the study of various bacteria. They identify and study the bacterias to apply its analysis in the betterment of medical science. Bacteriologist is a research based jobs in laboratories. In order to conduct independent research or work through a university, a Ph.D. is required after the Bachelor's degree.
