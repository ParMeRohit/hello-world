Instrumentation Engineering

Instrumentation is a collective term for measuring instruments that are used for indicating, measuring and recording physical quantities such as flow, temperature, level, distance, angle, or pressure. The term has its origins in the art and science of scientific instrument-making.

nstrumentation can refer to devices as simple as direct-reading thermometers, or as complex as multi-sensor components of industrial control systems. Today, instruments can be found in laboratories, refineries, factories and vehicles, as well as in everyday household use

Career After Instrumentation Engineering

Industrial Automation

Industrial Automation is the process of utilising the machineries to an extent to reduce human work to the most minimum or zero. It works towards completing a project or daily work through automated machines with less to no man power. As an Industrial Automation engineer, the responsibilities include to analyse and identify any bugs or quality issues in the systems, networks, applications, hardware and software that may hamper in the development, service or business processes.

Chemical industries

Chemical industries comprises of the companies that use chemical process and procedures to make chemicals. It uses raw materials such as fossil fuels,limestone, air, water, salt and sulfur to treat them chemically and produce materials for use. These products produced are mainly sold within the chemical industry use and later for general public use. Most chemical companies also produce plastic. Chemical engineers use their knowledge of science and mathematics to bring improvements in the equipment used in the processing chemicals.

Manufacturing industries

All companies that manufacture products using raw materials and using labour and machines, tools, chemical and biological processing, or formulation falls under the umbrella of Manufacturing industries. Manufacturing industries is defined as the branch of manufacture and trade based on the fabrication, processing, or preparation of products from raw materials and commodities. This includes all foods, chemicals, textiles, machines, and equipment. This includes all refined metals and minerals derived from extracted ores. This includes all lumber, wood, and pulp products.

Bio medical industries

Biomedical industries purpose is to manufacture and produce medical equipments and materials for the healthcare of humans and animals. Its prime job is to make available adequate supplies for diagnosis of health risks, and the prevention and treatment of illnesses. Biomedical engineers Design systems and products, such as artificial internal organs, artificial devices that replace body parts. They also Evaluate the safety, efficiency, and effectiveness of biomedical equipment.

Aeronautical and space science sector

Aeronautical and space science sector is one of the most powerful industries. This sector caters to markets such as missiles, space, military aircraft, commercial airliners and general aviation. This sector provides military and civil aerospace hardware to the world. This sector hires engineers, scientists and technicians in large numbers due to the emphasis given on research and development. This is an ever growing industry and job opportunities always being available for a qualified candidate.

Steel Manufacturing industry

Steel Manufacturing industry basically comprise of companies that work on making steel through the use of iron ore and/or scrap. During the process, all the impurities are removed from the iron to have a solid and quality steel. These companies not only manufacture about also bend, align, cut, weld and assemble metals using various tools as per the requirements. Data suggest that India could be the world’s second largest steel consumer in 2019*.

Oil and gas and petrochemical industries

Oil and gas industry also known as Petroleum industry undertakes the global processes of exploration, extraction, refining, transporting, and marketing of petroleum products. Fuel oil and gasoline being the largest volume products of the industry. Petrochemical is a chemical obtained from petroleum and natural gas hence petrochemical industry deals with the production and trade of petrochemicals.Reliance Industries is one of India's biggest industrial groupings and the largest private sector company.

Defense Research and Production Units

As stated by the government of India, the Department of Defence Research is headed by a Secretary, who is also the Scientific Adviser to the Raksha Mantri and its duty is to the Government on scientific aspects of military equipment and logistics and the formulation of research, design and development plans for equipment required by the Services. Similarly, the Department of Defence Production is headed by a Secretary who deals with matters pertaining to defence production, indigenisation of imported stores, equipment and spares, planning and control of departmental production units of the Ordnance Factory Board and Defence Public Sector Undertakings (DPSUs).

Software and hardware sectors

Software and hardware sectors both are different sectors yet work on the computer related process and problems hand in hand. While Hardware sectors mainly manufacture, repair and maintain the physical parts of a computer, contradicting this the software sectors work on computer programming, coding and database on the servers with the use of a computer. The combination of instrumentation’s knowledge and computer languages has proven to be very successful in these sectors. It is very essential to have knowledge regarding computer languages.
