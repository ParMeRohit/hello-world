Information Technology

Information technology (IT) is the use of computers to store, retrieve, transmit, and manipulate data, or information, often in the context of a business or other enterprise.

In an academic context, the Association for Computing Machinery defines IT as "undergraduate degree programs that prepare students to meet the computer technology needs of business, government, healthcare, schools, and other kinds of organizations

Career After Information Technology

IT Consultant

IT Consultants needs to at least have a Bachelor's Degree in computer science, information science, or a similar field for entry-level employment. Their job is basically to advise small and big firms with Information Technology to meet their business objectives or overcome problems. They could work on a contract or even as self-employed. An IT Consultant needs problem-solving and strategic planning ability along with analytical skills to excel in this Industry.

Cloud Architect

In order to manage a company's cloud storage, a Cloud Architect works on cloud application designs, cloud approval plans, and the required systems. They are the experts of Information Technology (IT) responsible for the supervision of a company's cloud computing system. Applicants must have a strong background of knowledge in scripting and programming languages. Cloud Architects work to help organizations avoid risk and ensure a successful, cost-effective transition to the cloud.

Computer Forensic Investigator

Computer Forensic Investigator job is a more Legal job than compared to any in the IT field. They are professionals who work very closely with law enforcement agencies, as well as private firms, to retrieve information from computers and other types of data storage devices. In this job, Computer Forensic Investigator has to search and save data found online in order to submit the same as evidence in court trials. They have to write structured reports acceptable for court based on the computer data. In some cases, they could be asked to give testimonies too.

Health IT Specialist

Health information technology (health IT) specialists handle the technical aspects of managing patient health information. A Health IT Specialist, is required to store patients health database and records in the computer on in any electronic form. They are also responsible for maintaining and managing the same Data. They could be posted in medical facilities or in doctor's offices. Some may also use coding system to enter Data in the organisation software for medical procedures or for future analysis and insurance billing purposes.

Mobile application manager

To enable and secures IT control over enterprise applications on end users' corporate and personal smartphones and tablets, companies hire Mobile application manager. Their job is to monitor a software application's performance and alert administrators if there is a problem. Most use the tool called Mobile Application Manager (MAM) to remotely install, update, remove, audit, and monitor software programs installed on smartphones and tablets.The position combines managerial responsibilities with in-depth hands-on technical expertise.

Web developer

Web developer is a specialized programmer who engage in the development of World Wide Web applications using a client–server model. While graduating choose a Development Specialization, develop Technical Skills, practice and train on Coding Skills in order to build a good portfolio and land a job as a Web developer. This job is to basically code, design and give the layout of a website according to a company's specifications.

Software Developer

Software developers in simple terms are the creative minds behind computer programing. Working in team with the IT department of a business, a Software Developer will be involved in collaboration with the Business Analysts and Development Managers to ensure software projects meet requirements.Some develop the applications that allow people to do specific tasks on a computer or another device. Others develop the underlying systems that run the devices or networks.

Information Technology vendor manager

Information Technology vendor managers make and maintain relationships with vendors while building the supply of materials and negotiating contracts with them for the betterment of the business. Vendor managers work under and report to sales managers. To understand what's best for the company, the Information Technology vendor managers need to be skilled in Maths and decision-making to select the right vendors that best fit the company's needs.

Geospatial professionals

Examination of Data from the geographical record including aerial photographs, GIS data, satellite data, soil analysis and other environmental samples, and any paper published about and within the landscape is what the Geospatial professionals do. A bachelor's degree in Geographic information systems (GIS), geography or computer science provides a strong foundation for a career as a Geospatial professional.

Data Modeler

Data Modelers work with data architects to design data models to improve efficiency and outputs, focus on issues such as reducing data redundancy or improving data movement across systems. They ensure the database meets the organizational needs using conceptual, logical, and physical data models Data models define how data is connected to each other and how they are processed and stored inside the system. Data modeler is responsible for the above defined context.
