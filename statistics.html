BSC in Statistics

The branch of mathematics that deals with the collection, organization, analysis, and interpretation of numerical data. Statistics is especially useful in drawing general conclusions about a set of data from a sample of the data. Used with a plural verb Numerical data.

Statistical knowledge helps you use the proper methods to collect the data, employ the correct analyses, and effectively present the results. Statistics is a crucial process behind how we make discoveries in science, make decisions based on data, and make predictions.

Career After BSC Statistics

Statisticians

Statisticians collect, analyse and interpret data to help solve problems or even get survey results in a company. This is a numerative calculation using statistical theories and methods on the collected data. To collect data, Statisticians create polls, surveys and questionnaires. Statistics is required in important fields such as business, engineering, science and other fields to understand the trends and forecast situations. A Statisticians could find jobs in Market research companies, Hospitals, Office for National Statistics (ONS), Industrial, pharmaceutical and commercial companies and other such sectors.

Business Analyst

Though Data Analyst and Business Analyst both deal with Data, what differentiates them is the application of the Data collected. Business Analyst uses the statistics of the collected Data to use it for the cost de effectiveness and improvement of business as a whole. Blake Angove, director of technology services at IT recruiting firm LaSalle Network says “Business analysts might take requirements from the business and work between the business and the technical team to develop a software package or implement a new CRM,”

Mathematician

The Scientific application of Mathematical principles to the collection, analysis and presentation of numerical Data is Statistics, hence Mathematicians are of core importance in any organisation to achieve business goals through the use of Statistics. A Mathematician's work is divided into types, i.e Theoretical (pure) mathematics and Applied mathematics, however the two often overlap. Mathematicians use mathematical theory, algorithms and computational techniques to solve business, engineering, physics, economic and scientific problems.

Professor

A teacher who teaches Post-secondary are often referred to as Professors.After the Bachelor's Degree, a professor is required to have a Ph.D or a Master's Degree in the relevant field. Apart from teaching students in college above High School, Professors' also conduct research and experiments to publish papers or academic journals. A professor also guides the students to choose the field of interest as per the students skills and capabilities. Professors need to be updated with the latest developments in their fields of study.

Risk analyst

A Risk Analyst usually are required and work in financial institutes such as banks and investment firms, however a risk analyst could be hired in the accounting departments of multinational companies too. They examine a company's investments using in-house software models or from a 3rd party vendor along with their analytical and statistical skills to analyse the risk involved and make recommendations to limit risk through diversification, currency exchanges and other investment strategies, mainly to avoid potential losses. After Graduation, additional experience in financial industry improves the chances of getting hired as a Risk Analyst.

Data analyst

Number-driven people with an interest in programming undertake jobs as Data Analyst. Data Analyst converts numerical data into simple and understandable charts or visual presentations to identify trends with the data collected through online and offline surveys, market research, logistics and sales figures. They help the company to make better decisions for profitable future using their statistics. Data Analysts are paid a good compensation compared to most other similar job profiles, even at entry level.

Content Analyst

A Content Analyst evaluates and interprets the given data. that can be in various profiles such as data translation, print media, product descriptions, advertising, web solutions etc. In simple terms, a writer writes content on topics in above mentioned fields and an analyst analyse that content. This role is to avoid grammatical errors and ensure accuracy before the content being published. They also analyse the content for being easy-to-read by the audience. Prime skills include good vocabulary with creativity and data management abilities.

Statistic trainer

Statistics being complex and vast field there is a huge requirement of Statistics trainer. To be a Statistic trainer one needs to know Data analysing thoroughly and have the skills to communicate and teach the same in an understandable manner. As a Statistic trainer one needs to know and stay updated with the latest developments in the Data Statistics field to share the knowledge with the students. There are courses to provide Statistics training.

Data scientist

Data scientist analyses 'big data' to give companies ideas to increase business and profits. In order to extract knowledge and interpret data from structured and unstructured data, Data scientist uses the scientific, algorithmic and programming methods. Data scientists are experts who have the skills to solve complex problems using statistics. They are multi-talented mathematicians and are a step above Statisticians and Data analyst.

Consultant

Consultation is sharing of knowledge and expertise on statistics to companies for solving problems and getting profitable future. A consultant can give his/her services to a company or even on a personal level. Experience and statistical skills are important for a consultant to be the best at what he does and to be approachable by companies. One of the perks of being a consultant is that one gets to work as a freelancer or self-employed.

Biostatistician

Biostatisticians analyze the data that is derived from living organisms during medical research studies and use the statistics to give advance knowledge in the field of science. This helps solve current important problems as well as prepare for the future. This role is important in a society and also being a biostatistician is a combination of employment stability, decent salary potential, and personal satisfaction. A Biostatisticians could work full time on research in labs or even as professors in Universities.

Benchmark

Calyampudi Radhakrishna Rao, FRS known as C R Rao (born 10 September 1920) is an Indian-American mathematician and statistician. He is currently professor emeritus at Pennsylvania State University and Research Professor at the University at Buffalo

Prasanta Chandra Mahalanobis OBE, FNA,FASc, FRS (29 June 1893 – 28 June 1972) was an Indian Bengali scientist and applied statistician. He is best remembered for the Mahalanobis distance, a statistical measure, and for being one of the members of the first Planning Commission of free India.

Karl Pearson has been credited with establishing the discipline of mathematical statistics. He founded the world's first university statistics department at University College, London in 1911, and contributed significantly to the field of biometrics and meteorology.
